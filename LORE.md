# Mountain Rise: The Lore

## The Setting Year 1650 - The Russian Mountains

You find yourself at the crossroads of three great civilizations: Russian Cossacks, Mongol Khans, and independent mountain tribes. The vast mountain ranges stretching from the Urals to the Altai have long been contested territories, with no single power holding dominion over all.

---

## Your Quest

As a warrior of considerable skill, you have been tasked with an ambitious mission: to unite the mountain territories under a single banner. Your reputation precedes you, and two legendary riders have volunteered to join your cause:

- **Aleksandr the Swift** - A Cossack rider from the southern plains, skilled in mounted combat
- **Temüjin the Sturdy** - A descendant of the great Mongol tribes, tough as the mountains themselves

Together, you will face the guardians and warlords of each region, proving your strength and claiming the lands for your growing empire.

---

## The Five Regions

### 1. Siberian Pass
**The Gateway to Fortune**

Your journey begins at the Siberian Pass, a narrow corridor through jagged peaks. Here, opportunistic bandits and raiders prey on travelers. These are the weakest opponents - a good place to gain experience and test your new companions' abilities.

**Key Enemies:**
- Mountain Bandits (petty thieves)
- Cossack Raiders (experienced fighters)
- Pirate Scouts (fast and cunning)

**Lore**: This pass has been a critical trade route for centuries, making it a haven for criminals.

---

### 2. Mongolian Plateau
**Land of the Khans**

The vast Mongol Plateau stretches endlessly, home to nomadic warriors and proud Mongol clans. Temüjin speaks of his ancestors roaming these lands. Here you face true warriors - experienced fighters who will test your growing abilities.

**Key Enemies:**
- Mongol Warriors (disciplined fighters)
- Nomadic Archers (deadly from range)
- Steppe Warlords (leaders of armies)

**Lore**: The legacy of Genghis Khan still echoes here. The greatest warriors of the Mongol tradition still defend these grasslands.

---

### 3. Ural Mountains
**The Ancient Peaks**

The Ural Mountains are old - older than memory. Shrouded in perpetual mist, they are home to shamans, herders, and mystical forces. Some say the mountains themselves resist those who would claim them.

**Key Enemies:**
- Mountain Shamans (mystical warriors)
- Yak Herders (strong and resilient)
- Avalanche Summoners (dangerous spellcasters)

**Lore**: Local tribes believe the Urals are sacred. They guard their territory fiercely against outsiders.

---

### 4. Lake Baikal
**The Deepest Water**

Lake Baikal, the world's deepest lake, is a land of mystery and extremes. Its waters are so clear you can see the bottom, and so cold they never fully thaw. Ice mages and frost giants dwell in these lands.

**Key Enemies:**
- Ice Wizards (magical combatants)
- Fish Merchants (struggling civilians)
- Frost Giants (titanic creatures of ice and malice)

**Lore**: Legends speak of an ancient civilization that once thrived on Baikal's shores before vanishing without a trace.

---

### 5. Altai Mountains
**The Final Challenge**

The Altai Mountains are the most formidable obstacle - home to the greatest warriors and most powerful beings. Only the truly skilled dare enter here. At the heart lies the legend that must not be: the Black Dragon Guardian.

**Key Enemies:**
- Golden Eagle Knights (elite warriors)
- Wolf Pack Leaders (coordinated and deadly)
- **Black Dragon Guardian** (the ultimate trial)

**Lore**: The Altai is rumored to contain the ancestral seat of power for all the mountain kingdoms. Controlling it means controlling the destiny of the entire region.

---

## Character Classes: The Three Mounts

### The Moose Warriors
**Mount**: Great Moose
**Strength**: Raw power and endurance
**Ability**: Deals significant damage, survives more hits
**Weakness**: Slower to move and react

Your moose carries you with unstoppable force. In combat, you are a battering ram - devastating when you connect, but somewhat slow.

### The Horse Riders
**Mount**: Swift Horse
**Strength**: Speed and balanced abilities
**Ability**: Fast movement, moderate damage, good defense
**Weakness**: Less extreme in any one area

Your horse is a classic choice. Neither the strongest nor the fastest, but exemplifying balance and versatility.

### The Caribou Travelers
**Mount**: Strong Caribou
**Strength**: Resilience and defense
**Ability**: Excellent defensive capabilities, steady stamina
**Weakness**: Lower offensive power

Your caribou is built for endurance. It shields you from most attacks and keeps you moving through harsh terrain.

---

## The Progression of Power

As you win battles and conquer territories, your power grows:

1. **Early Journey (Turns 1-5)**: Gain experience, level up fast, become confident
2. **Mid Journey (Turns 6-12)**: Face tougher opponents, develop strategy, explore all regions
3. **Late Journey (Turns 13-20)**: Reach peak power, challenge the mightiest foes, secure your legacy

---

## The Final Battle

After mastering 20 regions, you will have proven yourself a legend. Your name will be spoken with reverence in the mountain towns. Merchants will tell tales of your deeds. Warriors will aspire to match your skill.

But your ultimate legacy - controlling the full expanse of the Russian Mountains - depends on defeating every obstacle and never backing down.

---

## Historical Note

*This tale is inspired by the real struggles for power in Central Asia during the 16th-17th centuries, when Russian expansion met Mongol traditions in the contested mountain territories. While fictionalized, the spirit of these regions - their beauty, danger, and the clash of cultures - remains at the heart of Mountain Rise.*
